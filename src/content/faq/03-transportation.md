---
title: "Is transportation in Taiwan bad or dangerous?"
order: 3
---

Taiwan has a generally reliable and safe transportation system, but there are some challenges to be aware of—especially for pedestrians.

* **Public Transport:** Taiwan’s trains, high-speed rail, metro systems (MRT), and buses are modern, punctual, and safe. Most signage includes English, and ticketing is user-friendly.
* **Urban Traffic:** In cities, the streets can be busy, and scooters are very common. While overall road safety is good, pedestrian infrastructure can be lacking in some areas. Crosswalks may not always be respected by drivers or riders, so it’s important to stay alert when walking, even at green lights.
* **Driving:** Renting a car or scooter is possible with an international license. Roads are in good condition, but driving in cities or rural mountain areas can be challenging for first-time visitors.
* **Cycling & Walking:** Taiwan has many scenic bike paths, and bike-sharing is widely available. However, sidewalks in some city areas may be narrow, uneven, or shared with scooters.

In summary, Taiwan is safe and easy to travel around, but pedestrians should exercise extra caution in urban traffic.
