---
title: "Should I travel independently or join a tour when visiting Taiwan?"
order: 4
---

Both options work well in Taiwan, depending on your travel style.

* **Independent Travel:** You can take high-speed trains, local trains, buses, or the MRT (subway) in major cities. You can buy tickets online or at stations, and signs are often in English. Popular apps like Google Maps or the Taiwan Railway app make planning simple. If you prefer more freedom, you can also rent a car or scooter (an international license is required). Most attractions are easy to reach, and many locals are happy to help if you ask.
* **Group Tours:** Joining a tour is a good option if you prefer convenience, want to visit remote areas (like Alishan or Taroko Gorge), or don’t speak Chinese. Tours often include transportation, accommodation, and a guide who can explain local culture.
