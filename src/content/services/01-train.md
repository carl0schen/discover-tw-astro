Taiwan's railway journey reveals the island's soul through traditional tracks that wind through diverse landscapes. The classic [**TRA**](https://tip.railway.gov.tw/tra-tip-web/tip?lang=EN%5FUS) lines circle the entire island, connecting bustling urban centers with serene countryside. The charming narrow-gauge Alishan Forest Railway climbs through misty mountains and bamboo forests to spectacular sunrise viewing points. The nostalgic Pingxi Line takes you through tunnels and over bridges to quaint former mining towns. The dramatic East Coast line hugs cliffsides with breathtaking Pacific Ocean panoramas, while the Jiji Line meanders through rural villages and tea plantations. These historic railways offer both practical transportation and windows into Taiwan's cultural heritage.
